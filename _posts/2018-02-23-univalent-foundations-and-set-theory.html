--- layout: talk title: Logic Workshop talk_title: Univalent Foundations and Set Theory categories: logic-workshop date: 2018-02-23 semester: spring-2018 speaker_first: Dimitris speaker_last: Tsementzis speaker_website: http://rci.rutgers.edu/~dt506/ affiliation: Rutgers University abstract: "The Univalent Foundations is a proposed foundation for mathematics that takes as primitive a notion of space (rather than a notion of set). I will introduce the basic concepts of the Univalent Foundations from first principles, and give an overview of a class of formal systems, called Homotopy Type Theories, that can be used to formalize these basic notions. I will then describe how to construct a model of set theory in the Univalent Foundations, explain the consequences of the construction, and discuss several open problems. In the process, I will demonstrate the implementation of everything I talk about in a proof assistant." excerpt_separator: talk: yes note: ---
