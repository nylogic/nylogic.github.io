--- layout: talk title: Logic Workshop talk_title: Counting siblings categories: logic-workshop date: 2019-12-06 semester: fall-2019 speaker_first: Chris speaker_last: Laskowski speaker_website: "https://www.math.umd.edu/~laskow/" affiliation: University of Maryland abstract: "Two countable structures are siblings if each is embeddable into the other (e.g., any two countable non-scattered linear orders are siblings). Clearly if M and N are siblings, they have the same finite substructures, hence have the same universal theories. We characterize the universal theories in a finite, relational language that have a countable model with $2^{\aleph_0}$ siblings. This is joint work with Sam Braunfeld." excerpt_separator: talk: yes note: ---
