--- layout: talk title: MOPA talk_title: The pentagon lattice categories: MOPA date: 2019-12-04 semester: fall-2019 speaker_first: Athar speaker_last: Abdul-Quader speaker_website: "https://www.purchase.edu/live/profiles/2086-athar-abdul-quader" affiliation: Purchase College abstract: "Wilkie (1977) proved that if $M$ is a countable model of ${\rm PA}$, it has an elementary end extension $N$ such that the interstructure lattice $Lt(N / M)$ is the pentagon lattice $\mathbf{N}_5$. A similar result can be shown for cofinal extensions. Surprisingly, a new result by Jim Schmerl states that no model of ${\rm PA}$ has a 'mixed' elementary extension (one that is neither end nor cofinal) whose interstructure lattice is the pentagon. In this talk, I will go over the definitions and describe the method used in proofs about interstructure lattices." excerpt_separator: talk: yes note: ---
